## What is the Vacuum Pose?

The vacuum primarily targets the **transversus abdominis (TVA)** ‚Äî the deepest layer of your abdominal wall. Unlike the ‚Äúsix-pack‚Äù (rectus abdominis), which flexes the spine, the TVA acts more like a natural weight belt, pulling the internal organs inward and stabilizing the core.

**Why it matters:**
- **Waist control:** Helps prevent distension or ‚Äúbubble gut‚Äù on stage  
- **Aesthetic taper:** Narrows the waist from the front and side, enhancing the V-taper  
- **Mandatory element:** In Classic Physique, the vacuum is required in poses like the Front Double Biceps

## How to Perform the Vacuum

The goal is to pull your belly button as close to your spine as possible.

1. **Exhale fully**  
   Blow every last bit of air out of your lungs. This is the most important step ‚Äî without air in your lungs, the diaphragm can rise, allowing the stomach to hollow out.

2. **The draw-in**  
   Without inhaling, pull your navel inward and upward toward your ribcage. Think about trying to touch your spine with your belly button.

3. **Hold & breathe**  
   Once the vacuum is set, take very shallow ‚Äúsipping‚Äù breaths through your chest (not your stomach) to maintain the hold.

4. **Release**  
   Slowly let the air back in and relax the muscle.
